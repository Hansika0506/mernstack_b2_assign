The Solar System consists of the Sun and the astronomical objects gravitationally bound in orbit around it. The name comes from the Latin word Sōl, meaning Sun. The system formed about 4.6 billion years ago from a giant molecular cloud.

Formation and Evolution

The solar system formed when a molecular cloud collapsed and created a protoplanetary disk. The Sun formed at the center, and the remaining material coalesced into the planets, moons, and other bodies. Over time, gravitational interactions and collisions shaped the current structure of the system.

Planets

There are eight planets in the Solar System: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. They are divided into terrestrial planets (rocky) and gas giants (Jupiter and Saturn) or ice giants (Uranus and Neptune).

Exploration

Since the 20th century, space agencies like NASA and ESA have launched numerous missions to explore the planets and moons of the Solar System. Probes such as Voyager, Cassini, and rovers like Curiosity and Perseverance continue to send valuable data.
